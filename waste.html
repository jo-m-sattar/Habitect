Waste & Bioreactors

Transforming waste into resources through biotechnology

Genetic Engineering

Use CRISPR-Cas9 to introduce enzyme genes (e.g., PETase & MHETase) to break plastics.

Use E. coli or Pichia pastoris as test hosts to mass-produce enzymes for initial trials.

Bioreactor Design (Two-Stage)

Stage 1: Hydrolysis & acidogenesis — complex organics converted to volatile fatty acids (VFAs).

Stage 2: Methanogenesis — methanogens convert VFAs to methane (biogas).

Control: thermophilic temperature (~55 °C), pH 6.8–7.2, continuous mixing, gas capture.

Acceleration: auxiliary enzymes (cellulase, lipase), microbial carriers (biochar), sludge recirculation.

Integration

Liquid digestate filtered & diluted to produce nutrient solution for hydroponics (soilless agriculture).

Biogas can be used for power or fuel; CO₂ can feed plants in BLSS.
