Power Systems

Sustainable energy for long-duration space missions

Overview

Space systems rely on a mix of solar photovoltaics, radioisotope power systems, small fission reactors (Kilopower), and batteries. Design choices depend on mission profile (Earth orbit vs. deep space vs. planetary surface).

Solar Power

Space PV benefits from no atmosphere: higher irradiance and continuous sunlight in certain orbits.

Technologies: multi-junction GaAs, perovskite-silicon tandems, roll-out solar arrays (ROSA), tracking arrays.

Space-based solar power (SBSP) concept: build large orbital arrays, beam energy to Earth or base via microwave/laser.

Nuclear Power

Generations: SNAP-series, GPHS-RTG, MMRTG, NextGen RTG.

Kilopower / KRUSTY: small fission reactor designs to provide kW-scale continuous electrical power on planetary surfaces.

Energy Storage

Batteries: Li-ion are current standard for spacecraft; careful charge management is required to prevent overcharge or full deep discharge.

Emerging solid-state batteries (SSB) offer higher energy density and safety but face commercialization challenges.
