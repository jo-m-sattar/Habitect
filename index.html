The Challenge of Space Habitation

If your home had no air, no food, no water, and radiation was attacking you everywhere, how would you live? Welcome to space — an environment with invisible enemies. Temperature can swing to deadly extremes: +127 °C in sunlight and −173 °C in shadow on the Moon. Supplies such as oxygen, water, and food must be recycled or produced, because natural regeneration is impossible. Growing food in space requires soilless agriculture and controlled environment agriculture; water must be purified and recycled (urine recycling, humidity condensate recovery). Radiation (galactic cosmic rays & solar particle events) is a major threat; doses over 1,000 mSv risk severe health damage. The Habitect project shows integrated, redundant systems to keep humans safe and productive.
